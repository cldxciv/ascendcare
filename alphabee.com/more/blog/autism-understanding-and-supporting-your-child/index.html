What is Autism (ASD)?

Autism, or autism spectrum disorder (ASD), is a developmental disorder that affects how a person communicates, interacts, and experiences the world. Every child with autism is unique, with their own strengths and challenges. As a parent, understanding autism can help you better support your childs growth and development.

Common Signs of Autism

Children with autism may show certain signs, such as:

  • Difficulty with social interactions and communication
  • Limited eye contact or response when called by name
  • Repetitive behaviours or movements
  • Strong interest in specific topics or objects
  • Sensitivity to sounds, lights, or textures

These signs can vary widely. Some children may have mild challenges, while others may need more support.

Behaviours of Children with Autism

Children with autism might:

  • Have trouble understanding others feelings
  • Prefer to play alone
  • Use few or no words, or lose previously learned words
  • Follow strict routines and get upset by changes
  • Line up toys or objects in a specific way

These behaviours are part of how they experience the world. Understanding them can help you create a supportive environment.

What Causes Autism?

The exact cause of autism is unknown. Research suggests that genetics and environmental factors may play a role. Its important to know that autism is not caused by parenting or vaccines.

Is There a Cure for Autism?

Currently, there is no cure for autism. However, early intervention and appropriate support can improve a childs development and quality of life. The focus is on helping children build skills and manage challenges.

Can Autism Change Over Time?

Autism itself does not get worse with age, but challenges may change. Some children show improvements as they grow, while others may face new difficulties. Each childs journey is different, and early support can make a difference.

Autism and Sleep

Many children with autism have trouble sleeping. They may have difficulty falling asleep or wake up often during the night. Establishing a bedtime routine and creating a calm sleep environment can help improve sleep quality.

Can Autism Be Treated?

While autism cannot be cured, various therapies and interventions may help children develop skills and manage challenges. These approaches aim to support communication, social skills, and daily living abilities.

What Type of Therapy Is Best for Autism?

Theres no single therapy that works for every child. Some common approaches include:

  • Applied Behaviour Analysis (ABA): Focuses on teaching new skills and reducing challenging behaviours.
  • Speech Language Therapy: Helps improve communication skills.
  • Occupational Therapy: Assists with daily living skills and sensory issues.
  • Social Skills Groups: Teaches how to interact with others.


Its important to work with professionals to find the right combination of therapies for your child.

How Can You Support Your Child?

Every child with autism is unique. What works for one may not work for another. Early intervention and consistent support can make a big difference in your childs development. If you have concerns about your childs development, speak with your doctor or a specialist for guidance.
